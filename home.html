Welcome to PlayGhana, your gateway to Ghana's vibrant cultural heritage!
Explore and enjoy our collection of traditional games, passed down through generations. From the energetic 'Ampe' to the strategic 'Oware', experience the excitement and richness of Ghanaian culture. Watch, learn, and share in the fun with family and friends. Start your cultural journey now!

Ghana is a nation with several tribes, religions and traditions. All through the northern sphere to the southern is a wide range of several cultures with different traditons and cultural practices.

It is very sad the young ones of today know almost nothing about their traditions: traditional, games.

Technology in a way is replacing our traditions. Traditions in themselves are treasures in earthen vessels, and so should not be lost. We therefore sought to use Technology to promote the same traditions it is almost replacing in our daily lives.

I remember when we were kids and played games that made us happy and sometimes sad? Now, with phones and computers everywhere, we don't play like that anymore. Let's think back to those fun times and play those old games again. Those games were special because they taught us to work together, think carefully, and keep trying. I wish my kids could play like we did, but now they play with screens instead. I hope this post will help people remember the fun times and introduce those games to people who never played them. Let's make sure our kids know about these classic Ghanaian games too. Here's a list of some games we loved when we were young:

  • Oware
  • Pilolo
  • Antoakyire
  • Ampe
  • Tu Man Tu(hopscotch)
  • Day born
  • Ma te ma te
  • Sansankroma
  • Robert
  • Baabaabamlika

This website is aimed to promote Ghanaian rich cultures and traditions by portraying traditional dances and games. Surfing through this webiste, one would learn about the different dances and games about different cultures in Ghana.

HOW TRADITIONAL DANCES ENHACE THE SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS

Ghanaian traditional games can fall under several Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), but most directly under:

SDG 3, Good Health and Well-being: Traditional games promote physical activity, mental well-being, and social interaction, contributing to overall health and well-being.

SDG 4, Quality Education: Traditional games often teach valuable skills, such as strategy, teamwork, and problem-solving, which can complement formal education.

SDG 11, Sustainable Cities and Communities: Traditional games can foster community engagement, social cohesion, and cultural heritage, contributing to sustainable and inclusive communities.

SDG 17, Partnerships for the Goals: Revitalizing traditional games can involve partnerships between communities, organizations, and governments to preserve cultural heritage and promote sustainable development.

These SDGs align with the benefits of traditional games, including physical activity, social interaction, education, and cultural preservation.
